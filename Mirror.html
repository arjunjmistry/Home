Product: Mirror

Materials: Silver, glass, aluminium

Year: 2021

Study on mirror making using freeform silvering process on glass. An Alkaline-Silver solution is mixed together to form what’s known as Tollen’s Reagent, a villainous black liquid with a heavy scent of ammonia. Introducing glucose, a monosaccaride, into the liquid, causes elemental silver to precipitate out from the mixture, to slowly a mirrored lacquer on of the surface of the glass.

Making silver mirrors is not an exact process and a degree of experimentation is required with ratios of components, levels of dilution and types of reactive agents. By altering these parameters, each mirror may take on unique characteristics.
