Artist: Pet Deaths

Venue: Southbank Centre

Direction: Stage Lighting

Photography by @theredbeanienz

Year: 2020

I first met frontmen Liam and Graeme in a pub in Islington. Behind the awkwardly polite small-talk that comes with meeting new people, in the background, a friend had just received news of the passing of someone close. on TV, a virus had caused havoc on an unknown population in an unconfirmed location. Drawing the outlines of the venue stage in my notebook, I started taking notes on where they drew their inspiration from.

Their topics of death, life and love, formed the backdrop for the next few months whilst developing a stage lighting strategy which reflected the storylines from their new album. With street lights invading my brand new curtainless flat, I considered knife-edge beams of light to capture the starkness of ‘Bats In Your Hair’. Watching traffic lights go red, smeared by the dew on the bus window a colour story was developed which reflected the setlist’s tidally shifting moods. Finally, in the laundrette when my machine broke down, a storyboard was assembled using the colour, texture and intensity of light as the medium.

Storyboard and lighting direction for Pet Death’s concert at Purcell Room, Southbank Centre. Working with the in-house lighting department to choreograph lighting scenes live on the night.
